Best Filipino Restaurants in Quezon City

Discover the top 10 local-favorite Filipino restaurants in Quezon City, from heritage classics to modern comfort food. These spots offer authentic flavors and memorable dining experiences that showcase the best of Filipino cuisine.

In this guide, we highlight the best Filipino restaurants in Quezon City — based on popularity, customer reviews, and unforgettable dining experiences. From traditional adobo to modern fusion dishes, here's where to eat authentic Filipino food in QC.

Curated by local food experts

Welcome to Filipino Cuisine: Quezon City's Heritage

Filipino cuisine is a beautiful blend of indigenous flavors, Spanish influences, and Asian techniques, creating dishes that are uniquely Filipino and deeply satisfying. From traditional adobo and sinigang to modern fusion interpretations, Quezon City offers some of the best Filipino dining experiences in Metro Manila.

1. Some Thai

₱₱₱

195 Tomas Morato Ave, Brgy. Laging Handa

Thai
Asian
Fine Dining

Must-try Dishes

  • Tom Yum - Authentic Thai hot and sour soup
  • Pad Thai - Classic stir-fried rice noodles
  • Crab Curry - Rich and creamy curry with fresh crab
  • 48-hour Beef Short Rib Curry - Slow-cooked perfection
Insider Tip

Best visited with friends so you can try multiple dishes family‑style.

Some Thai has quickly become a must-visit spot for Thai food lovers in Quezon City. Its vibrant interiors and authentic flavors make it perfect for group dining. The Tom Yum packs just the right balance of spice and tang, while their crab curry is a rich, indulgent treat.

2. Saigon Corner PH

₱

22 Scout Bayoran, South Triangle

Vietnamese
Asian
Casual

Must-try dishes

  • Pho - Seafood, vegan, coconut chicken varieties
  • Banh Mi - Fresh Vietnamese sandwiches
  • Banh Xeo - Crispy Vietnamese crepes
Insider Tip

Pet‑friendly spot — great for a relaxed lunch with your furry friend.

Saigon Corner is a cozy Vietnamese gem along Tomas Morato offering fresh, flavorful dishes at budget‑friendly prices. Their pho broth is light yet flavorful, and their banh mi sandwiches have the perfect crunch.

3. Azadore

₱₱₱

111 Scout Fernandez corner Scout Torillo St.

Mediterranean

Perfect for date nights — try their paella with a bottle of wine.

Azadore is celebrity chef Tatung's take on alfresco dining with Spanish and Mediterranean influences. It's a stylish yet casual place to enjoy hearty meals under the warm QC night sky.
